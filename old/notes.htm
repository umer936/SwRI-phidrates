Photoabsorption Cross Sections



Units:

Column 2 of the data files contain either cross sections (total absorption)
or branching ratios for sub-processes.

Wavelengths (column 1) are in Angstroms.
Cross sections (column 2) are in cm2.
Branching Ratios (column 2) are dimensionless.

Naming Conventions:

The file names are identified as:

parent-species__final-species.dat

parent-species__Total.dat is the total photoabsorption cross section for parent-species.

The tilde (~) denotes an excited state. For example, CO~a denotes CO(a), and C2H2~ denotes excited C2H2 (usually written as C2H2*). The characters "(", ")", and "*" are reserved by the operating system shell.

For atoms, electronic states are denoted as in: C1D = C(1D), O1S = O(1S), S3P = S(3P), etc.

Reference:

Solar Photo Rates for Planetary Atmospheres and Atmospheric Pollutants,

  • Huebner, W.F., J. J. Keady, and S. P. Lyon, Astrophys. Space Sci., 195, 1992.
